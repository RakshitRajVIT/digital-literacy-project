# Case Study: Online Payment Fraud and UPI


In India, UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a fast-growing cybercrime in which perpetrators deceive users into sending money or disclosing private banking information. Instead of using technical hacking, social engineering techniques are typically used in this kind of fraud.

One typical technique is for the scammer to pretend to be a customer or service provider. In a real-world example, Rahul, a college student, put an item up for sale on the internet. He received a phony "payment request" link via a messaging app from a con artist posing as interested. Rahul was told by the con artist to "enter his UPI PIN to receive money." Rahul entered his PIN in confusion but trust, unintentionally approving a payment rather than receiving one.₹15,000 was taken out of his bank account in a matter of seconds.

The steps usually include: <br/>  (1) calling or messaging the victim;  <br/> (2) establishing trust by using urgency or a false identity; <br/>  (3) sending a malicious link or QR code; and  <br/> (4) deceiving the victim into entering their OTP or UPI PIN.

Students, the elderly, and those who are not familiar with digital payment systems are typically the targets. Financial loss, psychological strain, and a decline in confidence in online systems are among the repercussions.

This instance emphasizes how crucial awareness is. Users should keep in mind that a UPI PIN is only needed to send money—not to receive it—and that it should never be shared.
